I've been dabbling with this project for a little while now, and seeing as the on-again-off-again Special Council meeting to discuss the fate of the proposed Toronto Casino is officially on again for Tuesday, I thought it might be timely to post this in advance of the final debate.

You can find the original data and all documentation related to the Toronto Casino Consultation efforts on the Toronto Open Data site.

From the City's Website:

"In November 2012, City Council's Executive Committee requested the City Manager to seek the public's input on the establishment of a casino in Toronto. The City Manager engaged the firm DPRA to conduct the consultation with the public and stakeholders by the end of January 2013. Public consultation is an important part of Council's decision making process. The consultation gathered input, including the public's views and opinions on a casino generally, on possible locations for a casino, and what the public would like Council to consider when making a decision on this matter."

The full consultant report on the outcome of the Casino Public Consultations is available here. After reading this report, however, I felt like that there was more to the story than was presented. This is often the case with the case with large datasets, and especially true when you introduce comment fields to a survey for people to freely contribute to.

Even now, after I've done my own round of analysis and visualization, I feel like there is still more to understand about how Torontonians really feel about the prospect of a new casino.

This project was designed so that I could teach myself how to use the Tableau Public software, so it relies heavily on Tableau visualizations.

I hope this helps to illuminate some of the many differing opinions and concerns about a GTA Casino that people have expressed through the pubilc consultation process.

Money Matters?

One of the conditions being considered at Council is the minimum acceptable annual income that the City would require is a new Casino is approved. THe figure currently being considered is at least $100 million.

Learn About Tableau

The survey results suggest that, of the people in support of a Casino, most are indifferent to how much the City actually makes in revenue, or do not require the $100 million dollars in revenue a year that is being asked for. Only 9.26% of survey respondents indicated that the City should only accept a Casino if at least $100 million dollars in revenue is guaranteed
